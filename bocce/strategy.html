🧠 Strategy

Play smarter, win more

The Two Shots
🎳 Pointing (Lagging)
  • Roll softly to land near the pallino
  • Best when you're already winning the frame
  • Lower risk — ball stays in play
  • Aim for the same line every time
  • Use a consistent underhand roll
💥 Hitting (Spocking/Raffa)
  • Throw hard to knock opponent balls away
  • Best when opponent is close to the pallino
  • High risk, high reward
  • Can also move the pallino to your advantage
  • Requires consistent release point
Key Strategy Tips
📍
Control the Pallino
The pallino's position dictates everything. When hitting, consider where the pallino will end up, not just where the opponent's ball will go. Knocking the pallino into a cluster of your own balls can flip a losing frame into a winning one.
Advanced
🎯
Establish a Line Early
Find your delivery line — the path from your feet to the pallino — and stick to it. Most pointing errors come from inconsistent angles, not inconsistent speed. Once your line is dialed in, adjusting distance becomes easy.
Fundamentals
🛡️
Block with Your Own Balls
When you're winning the frame, roll your next ball between the pallino and the opponent's approach lane. A well-placed blocker forces your opponent to either hit through it (risky) or go around it (less accurate). Defense wins bocce.
Tactics
🏃
Know When to Spock
Hitting makes sense when the opponent has 2+ balls close to the pallino and pointing can't close the gap. But if you miss a hit, you've given up a throw — so only spock when the math clearly favors it. When in doubt, point.
Decision Making
🧘
Manage the Score, Not the Moment
If you're up 10–3, don't take risks chasing a 4-point frame — play conservatively. If you're down 11–4, you need aggressive hitting. Let the score shape your risk tolerance. Bocce is a game of frames, not individual shots.
Mental Game
🌀
Use the Backwall (Where Legal)
In walled courts, the back wall is your friend. A ball cushioned off the back wall often ends up in a better position than a direct roll. Practice bank shots — they're legal and can be surprisingly accurate once you learn the rebound.
Court Awareness
👀
Watch Your Opponents' Tendencies
Some players always hit, some always point. If your opponent always spocks, block their line and let them knock your blocker farther into a scoring position. If they only point, place your balls tight and trust they won't take risks to remove them.
