📖 Helpful Terms

Bocce vocabulary you need to know

PallinoThe small target ball (also called jack or boccino) that players aim for throughout the frame
Bocce BallOne of the four larger balls each team throws toward the pallino — typically 107mm in diameter
FrameOne round of play in which all 8 bocce balls are thrown; similar to an inning in baseball
Pointing (Lagging)Rolling a bocce ball softly to land as close to the pallino as possible without hitting opponent balls
Spocking (Hitting)Throwing a bocce ball hard to knock an opponent's ball away from the pallino
RaffaA hitting shot thrown through the air (aerial) that lands and slides into the target — common in Italian-style bocce
VoloA high-arc aerial shot that lands directly on the target ball with little roll — requires precise release
InA bocce ball that is inside a court boundary and counts for scoring purposes
OutA bocce ball that has crossed a boundary line and is removed from the frame
Dead BallA ball removed from play for the frame, usually due to a foul, going out of bounds, or an invalid pallino throw
Foul LineThe line at each end of the court that players must not step on or over when throwing
BankerA ball that ricochets off the side or back wall before settling near the pallino — legal in walled courts
KissWhen a bocce ball gently touches and rests directly against the pallino — an ideal pointing shot
MeasureUsing a tape measure to determine which ball is closest to the pallino when it cannot be determined by eye
Score (Point)1 point awarded per bocce ball that is closer to the pallino than the opponent's nearest ball
Nullo (Zero Frame)A frame where no points are awarded because the two closest balls are equidistant from the pallino
RolinoInformal term for when the pallino is thrown softly along the ground rather than in the air — common in casual play
CourtThe rectangular playing surface; can be dirt, sand, grass, or packed clay — typically 60–90 ft × 10–13 ft in formal play
